# Image style — THE STORY OF EVERYTHING

Painterly cinematic realism throughout; the art should feel like one production even
as themes shift. 16:9, generated ≥1920px wide, **no text, no diagrams, no logos**.

Per-theme direction (keys match `era_art_styles` in channel.yaml):

- **cosmos** — deep-space astronomical art: rich blacks, luminous nebulae and
  structure, Hubble-palette blues/golds. Scale cues welcome (a probe, a telescope
  mirror, an observatory dome dwarfed by sky).
- **deep_time** — geological grandeur: canyon strata, ancient shorelines, volcanic
  horizons; 19th-century survey-expedition painting mood; warm mineral palette.
- **discovery** — the human scenes: candlelit studies, brass instruments, chalk
  boards, observatory interiors, period-accurate dress; Vermeer/de la Tour light.
  Faces allowed but not portrait-close (consistency across images is hard); favor
  figures-in-scene over closeups.
- **life** — natural-history richness: Haeckel-detailed organisms, primordial seas,
  microbial mats, deep greens and ambers.
- **human** — paleolithic and ice-age scenes: firelight, tundra light, muted ochres.

Consistency rules:
- One palette family per EPISODE (the brief names it); themes above modulate within it.
- Same painterly finish across all images — no style jumps between photoreal and flat.
- Compositions must survive a 20s static hold: strong single subject, depth, light
  direction; avoid busy symmetric wallpaper.
- Period accuracy matters (instruments, dress, technology) — anachronisms in the
  visuals undermine the channel's whole premise. When unsure, favor suggestion over
  specificity.
